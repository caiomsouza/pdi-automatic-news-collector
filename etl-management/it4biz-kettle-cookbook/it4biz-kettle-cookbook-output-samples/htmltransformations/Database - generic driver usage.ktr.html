A sample for a generic database driver usage.

See the Connections on the left for the example of using a MySQL driver (normally in standard, just for this example).
Look at the "Generic" tab on the connection and change the URL and Driver class according to your driver.
Don't forget to change your username & password on the "General" tab ;-)

ATTENTION: Remember to add the supplied database driver JAR to the classpath in your Spoon.bat / Pan.bat / Kitchen.bat / Chef.bat
like "set CLASSPATH=%CLASSPATH%;libext\mysql-connector-java-3.1.13-bin.jar"

If you need a special DLL or something like this, it must be placed in the directory "libswt\win32" instead of "windows\system32".

For Unix/Linus users the jars will be automatically addded to the classpath.

Press "Test" in your Connection or run a simple test like this below (remember to change the table name) by previewing one of the steps.
